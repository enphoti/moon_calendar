About this calendar

Each month begins on the evening the first crescent of the new moon becomes visible in the sky—approximately one and a half days after astronomical new moon. Months are 29 or 30 days long, following the natural synodic cycle of 29.53 days. A new year begins with the month whose new moon falls in December, so that January always falls within a named year. The small blue dot marks the approximate day of new moon within each month. Astronomical calculations follow the algorithms of Jean Meeus.
